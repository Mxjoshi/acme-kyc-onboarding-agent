# Acme Bank UAE - Customer Onboarding, KYC and AML Policy (v1.0)

SYNTHETIC DOCUMENT - NOT REAL. Written for a buildathon demo. Acme Bank is a fictional UAE bank.
Do not use for any real onboarding decision. A deliberate gap exists (remote onboarding of tourists
and virtual asset customers are intentionally not covered) so the assistant's refusal can be demoed.

---

## Section 1. Purpose and scope
1.1 This policy sets the rules for onboarding new individual customers to Acme Bank in the UAE.
1.2 It covers identity verification (KYC), anti money laundering checks (AML), required documents,
risk rating, and when a case must be escalated to a human officer.
1.3 It applies to all onboarding officers and to any assistant tool that supports them.

## Section 2. Definitions
2.1 Resident: an individual holding a valid UAE residence visa and Emirates ID.
2.2 Non-resident: an individual without a UAE residence visa.
2.3 Newcomer: a resident whose UAE residence began within the last 24 months.
2.4 PEP (politically exposed person): an individual entrusted with a prominent public function.

## Section 3. Customer categories
3.1 Acme Bank onboards UAE residents and non-resident individuals who visit a branch in person.
3.2 Each customer must be placed in exactly one category before any account is opened.
3.3 Newcomers are onboarded under the resident rules in Section 4.2, with the AML attention in
Section 6.

## Section 4. Required documents
4.1 General: every applicant must present a valid passport. The passport must not be expired.
4.2 Residents: in addition to the passport, a resident must present (a) a valid Emirates ID,
(b) a valid UAE residence visa, and (c) proof of UAE address.
4.3 Non-residents: in addition to the passport, a non-resident must present a proof of overseas
address and a reference letter from an existing bank.
4.4 Minimum age: the applicant must be at least 18 years old. Applicants under 18 cannot be onboarded.
4.5 Address verification: UAE address is verified by a tenancy contract (Ejari) or a recent utility
bill in the applicant's name. Overseas address is verified by a recent utility bill or bank statement.
4.6 Document validity: any document presented must be valid (not expired) on the date of onboarding.

## Section 5. Identity verification
5.1 The name and date of birth on the passport, Emirates ID and visa must match.
5.2 If the documents do not match, the case must be escalated to a human officer (Section 7).
5.3 The Emirates ID number must be recorded and stored in masked form.

## Section 6. AML risk rating
6.1 Every applicant is assigned a risk band of low, medium or high before onboarding.
6.2 Low risk: a salaried resident from a non-sanctioned country, employed by a known employer,
with no PEP status and no sanctions match.
6.3 Medium risk: self-employed applicants, or applicants with incomplete employment information.
Medium risk customers may be onboarded but require enhanced monitoring.
6.4 High risk: any applicant with a sanctions match, PEP status, or a connection to a sanctioned
country. High risk applicants must be escalated to a human officer and cannot be auto-onboarded.
6.5 Sanctions screening: every applicant's name must be screened against the sanctions list. A
positive match is treated as high risk under Section 6.4.

## Section 7. Escalation and human review
7.1 A case must be escalated to a human officer when any of the following is true:
(a) documents are missing or do not match (Section 5.2),
(b) the applicant is high risk (Section 6.4),
(c) the applicant is a PEP,
(d) the policy does not clearly cover the applicant's situation.
7.2 The assistant must never approve a case that meets an escalation trigger.
7.3 The human officer makes the final onboarding decision in all escalated cases.

## Section 8. Decision outcomes
8.1 Proceed: all required documents are present and valid, identity matches, and risk is low or
medium with monitoring. The customer can be onboarded.
8.2 Request documents: the applicant is otherwise acceptable but one or more required documents are
missing or expired. Onboarding pauses until the documents are provided.
8.3 Escalate: any escalation trigger in Section 7.1 applies. The case goes to a human officer.
8.4 The assistant must state which outcome applies and cite the section that supports it.

## Section 9. Record keeping and audit
9.1 For every onboarding decision, Acme Bank records the documents seen, the risk band, the outcome,
and the policy sections cited.
9.2 Records must be retained for at least five years and be available for audit.
9.3 Every decision must be explainable: a human must be able to see why the outcome was reached.
